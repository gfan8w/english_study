**dotard** /ˈdəʊtəd/  n.老糊涂；年老昏愦之人
A dotard is an old, senile person who is weak, confused, or has experienced a decline in their mental faculties.
The word originates from the Middle English word doten (meaning "to dote",vt.溺爱, dote(to be foolish,中古英语) with 
the suffix -ard, the "ard" suffix that means "too much") and is traditionally 
used as a **derogatory**(/dɪˈrɑːɡətɔːri/,adj.贬低的，贬义的)insult to describe an elderly person 
experiencing a "second childhood(老年糊涂,第二童年,老年智力衰退期)" or loss of intellect(n.智力).
